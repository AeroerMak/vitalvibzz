What Does It Do?

- Neotonics was created with the purpose of enhancing cell turnover within the skin. According to a board-certified dermatologist cited in the source, skin cell turnover refers to the regular shedding and replacement of skin cells with newer ones. Failure to perform this process regularly can lead to a buildup of dead skin on the surface, potentially causing various skin problems such as acne, hyperpigmentation, milia, and uneven skin texture.

To fully grasp the significance of skin cell turnover, we must start at the beginning, as stated by the same source. It is worth noting that the typical life span of regular skin cells is approximately 28 days. Put simply, these fresh cells originate in the deepest dermis layer (also referred to as the basal layer) and then travel to the surface. Subsequently, they enter a dormant phase and remain as a layer of deceased cells. In essence, every 28 days, this process of shedding the dead skin cells and being replaced occurs. As one ages, the overall turnover rate increases, resulting in more noticeable visible signs of aging.
